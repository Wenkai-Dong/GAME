GAME: Gated Attention-based Map Encoding for Humanoid Locomotion over Discrete Terrain

Abstract

Deep reinforcement learning has enabled humanoid robots to traverse a broad range of complex terrains with the aid of terrain perception. Yet narrow terrains that admit only a single feasible path, such as balance beams and single-file stepping stones, remain a long-standing challenge for perceptive locomotion, since one misplaced footstep there ends in a fall. Conventional terrain encoders mix abundant irrelevant signals from the surroundings into the representation and struggle to bring the sole viable foothold region into sharp focus. This paper presents Gated Attention-based Map Encoding, or GAME, an end-to-end perceptive locomotion framework for humanoid robots. Central to GAME are two complementary mechanisms embedded in query-driven attention. Channel-wise gating modulates the terrain representation at the attention output in a data-dependent, continuous manner, amplifying the feature channels that carry foothold cues while suppressing distracting ones, so that the encoding concentrates on the decisive foothold regions. Channel-wise attention pooling condenses the entire terrain into a global context vector at the encoder input and appends it to the query, letting the policy localize footholds under a global view of the terrain. Experiments on the Unitree G1 humanoid with 29 degrees of freedom show that GAME delivers robust locomotion at 1.5 m/s across diverse discrete terrains without sacrificing performance on conventional ones.
